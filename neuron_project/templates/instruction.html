Tips for Teachers

Talk to parents: Parents are often the first to notice signs of a neurological disorder in their child. Ask parents about their child's development, their behavior, and any concerns they may have. Observe children in the classroom: Pay attention to children's behavior, their interactions with others, and their ability to complete tasks.
Be aware of red flags: There are some red flags that may indicate a neurological disorder, such as:

  • • Delayed speech or language development
  • • Difficulty with fine or gross motor skills
  • • Attention problems
  • • Hyperactivity
  • • Impulsivity
  • • Difficulty with social interactions
  • • Repetitive or restrictive behaviors
  • • Seizures
If you are cncerned that a child may have a neurological disorder, it is important to talk to your child's teacher or another school staff member. They can help to connect you with the appropriate resources.
