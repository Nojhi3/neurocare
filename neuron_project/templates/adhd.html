ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder):

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity, significantly impacting daily functioning. It typically manifests in childhood and can persist into adolescence and adulthood.
What are the symptoms?

  • Careless Mistakes: Child makes frequent errors in schoolwork due to oversight.
  • Easily Distracted: Difficulty maintaining focus; easily sidetracked.
  • Difficulty Following Instructions: Struggles to adhere to given directions.
  • Inattentiveness When Spoken to Directly:Appears not to listen when directly addressed.
  • Organization Challenges: Difficulty organizing tasks and belongings
  • Incomplete Work: Often fails to complete assignments or chores.
  • Avoidance of Mental Effort:Resists tasks requiring sustained mental effort, including homework.
  • Loss of Items: Frequently misplaces homework, books, jackets, backpacks, or sports equipment.

Consult a Doctor: If parents observe a persistent and significant presence of these behaviors, adversely affecting the child's academic, social, and daily life functioning, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional for a comprehensive assessment and guidance on appropriate management strategies. Early intervention and support can contribute to better outcomes for children with ADHD.

How to identify ADHD



Simon Says

•This classic game can help assess a child's ability to listen, follow instructions, and stay focused. Watch for impulsive behavior, such as jumping up without waiting for the "Simon says" command


Age

3 to 5 years

Benefits

Memory, concentration and social skills.

Suitable for

Speech and Language delay, learning disability

Sorting and Matching Games

•Provide children with a set of objects to sort or match. This activity can help assess attention to detail, organization, and the ability to complete a task.

Benefits

Cognitive skills, coordination.

Obstacle Course

Create a simple indoor obstacle course with different activities like crawling under tables, jumping over cushions, or walking along a line. Observe how well the child follows the course, as well as any signs of impulsivity or difficulty with  .


Age

3 to 7 years

Benefits

Motor skills, teamwork, coordination.

Suitable for

Motor skills, teamwork, fun



Sensory Play

•Set up sensory bins with items like rice, beans, sand, or textured fabrics. Allow the child to explore and manipulate these materials. Sensory play can help children with ADHD regulate their sensory input and improve focus.


Age

2 to 7 years

Benefits

Development, exploration, stimulation

Suitable for

Child development, exploration, learning.
